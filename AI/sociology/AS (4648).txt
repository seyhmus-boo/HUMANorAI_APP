Globalization, characterized by intensified interconnectedness and interdependence across national borders, has profoundly impacted local cultures worldwide.  This paper investigates the multifaceted effects of globalization on the preservation and transformation of local cultural practices, traditions, and identities.  While proponents argue globalization fosters cultural exchange and enriches local expressions, critics highlight the homogenizing pressures leading to cultural erosion and the dominance of globalized norms. This study examines this complex interplay through a critical lens, analyzing the diverse ways in which globalization's economic, technological, and ideological dimensions interact with pre-existing cultural landscapes.
