Class inequality, the unequal distribution of resources and opportunities across social strata, profoundly impacts societal well-being.  Recent developments underscore its persistent and evolving nature.  While traditional Marxist analyses focused on ownership of the means of production, contemporary research emphasizes the multifaceted nature of class, incorporating factors like education, occupation, and social capital.  This expanded view reveals how inequality manifests not just in stark economic disparities but also in unequal access to healthcare, quality education, and political influence.

The consequences are far-reaching. High levels of inequality correlate with increased crime rates, poorer public health outcomes, and reduced social mobility.  Furthermore, recent studies highlight its corrosive effect on democratic institutions, fostering political polarization and undermining social cohesion.  The COVID-19 pandemic exacerbated existing inequalities, disproportionately impacting lower socioeconomic groups and highlighting the fragility of social safety nets.
