Class inequality, a persistent feature of human societies, continues to exert profound and multifaceted effects on social structures and individual well-being. This paper examines the pervasive influence of class inequality across various societal dimensions, exploring its impact on health outcomes, educational attainment, political participation, and social mobility.  We argue that class inequality is not merely a matter of income disparity but rather a complex interplay of economic, social, and political factors that reinforce existing power structures and limit opportunities for social advancement.
