Education plays a pivotal role in shaping and reinforcing social norms, acting as a crucial agent of socialization beyond the family unit.  Historically, schools transmitted dominant cultural values and beliefs, often implicitly.  However, recent developments highlight a shift towards more explicit and critical engagement with norm formation. Simultaneously, increased emphasis on diversity and inclusion initiatives aims to deconstruct entrenched biases and promote acceptance of diverse perspectives, directly challenging traditional social hierarchies and power dynamics.  Furthermore, advancements in digital literacy education equip students to critically analyze information disseminated online, mitigating the spread of misinformation and harmful ideologies that can reinforce negative social norms.  This multifaceted approach, moving beyond mere transmission of knowledge to active engagement with values and critical thinking, signifies a proactive role for education in shaping a more equitable and just society by fostering a generation capable of consciously constructing and reconstructing social norms.
