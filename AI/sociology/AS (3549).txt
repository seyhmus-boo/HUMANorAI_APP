The evolution of gender roles in modern societies constitutes a complex and multifaceted phenomenon demanding an interdisciplinary approach for its thorough understanding. This research paper examines the shifting dynamics of gender roles across various societal sectors, drawing upon insights from sociology, anthropology, psychology, and economics.  While traditional gender roles, often rooted in historical and cultural norms, continue to exert influence, contemporary societies witness significant transformations driven by factors such as technological advancements, economic globalization, and evolving social movements. We analyze the interplay between macro-level societal structures and micro-level individual experiences, considering the persistent inequalities despite progress towards gender equality.  The interdisciplinary lens allows for a nuanced exploration of the interplay between legal frameworks, cultural representations, individual agency, and the enduring power of ingrained biases, ultimately contributing to a more comprehensive understanding of this dynamic and ongoing social process.
