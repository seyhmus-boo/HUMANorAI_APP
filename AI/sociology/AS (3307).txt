Existing literature overwhelmingly demonstrates a strong correlation between socioeconomic status and access to quality education, healthcare, and housing, creating a self-perpetuating cycle of disadvantage.  This disparity manifests in stark health outcomes, with lower-income individuals experiencing higher rates of morbidity and mortality due to factors ranging from environmental hazards to limited access to preventative care.  Furthermore, class inequality fuels social unrest and political instability, as marginalized groups struggle for representation and equitable resource allocation.  Critically,  research highlights the detrimental effects on social cohesion, fostering distrust and hindering community development.
