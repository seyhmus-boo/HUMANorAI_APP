Good morning. Today's lecture addresses class inequality and its societal ramifications.  Class inequality, the unequal distribution of resources and opportunities based on socioeconomic status, profoundly impacts societal well-being.  Its effects are multifaceted, manifesting in disparate health outcomes, limited educational attainment for lower classes, and reduced social mobility.  

Individuals from disadvantaged backgrounds often experience poorer health due to factors like limited access to healthcare and increased stress associated with financial insecurity.  Educational disparities, stemming from unequal access to quality schooling and resources, perpetuate the cycle of inequality across generations.  Consequently, social mobility, the ability to move between socioeconomic classes, is significantly constrained, solidifying existing inequalities.

Understanding these effects has practical applications.  Policy interventions, such as progressive taxation, robust social safety nets, and investment in equitable education, can mitigate these inequalities.  Furthermore, promoting economic policies that foster inclusive growth and combat discriminatory practices are crucial steps towards creating a more just and equitable society.
