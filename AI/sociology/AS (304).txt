Historically, rigid gender roles, largely dictated by patriarchal structures, dominated most societies.  Men were predominantly associated with public life, economic production, and political power, while women were confined to the domestic sphere, responsible for childcare and household management. This division of labor, often justified through religious or cultural ideologies, persisted for centuries.  However, the 20th and 21st centuries have witnessed significant shifts in these traditional norms.  The two world wars, for instance, necessitated increased female participation in the workforce, challenging established gender hierarchies.  The subsequent feminist movements of the latter half of the 20th century actively challenged patriarchal structures, advocating for equal rights and opportunities.
