This research paper examines the multifaceted relationship between cultural diversity and social integration within contemporary societies. While increasing cultural diversity is often presented as a societal benefit, promoting dynamism and innovation, its impact on social cohesion remains a subject of ongoing debate. This study explores how diverse cultural backgrounds influence various aspects of social integration, focusing on the mediating roles of factors such as intergroup contact, prejudice and discrimination, and the availability of social support networks.
