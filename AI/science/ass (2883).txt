Carbon nanotubes (CNTs), possessing exceptional mechanical, electrical, and thermal properties, represent a unique material poised to revolutionize diverse scientific fields. This presentation will explore the key properties of CNTs, emphasizing their interdisciplinary significance.  We will examine their remarkable tensile strength and elasticity, surpassing that of steel, alongside their tunable electrical conductivity, ranging from metallic to semiconducting behavior depending on chirality.  Their high thermal conductivity makes them ideal for thermal management applications.

The interdisciplinary nature of CNT research will be highlighted through examples.  Their exceptional mechanical properties are driving advancements in materials science and engineering, leading to the development of novel composites and high-performance fibers.  Their electrical properties are crucial for electronics and sensor technologies, enabling the creation of flexible and transparent displays, as well as highly sensitive biosensors.  Furthermore, their biomedical applications, ranging from drug delivery to tissue engineering, will be briefly discussed, showcasing their potential in bridging the gap between nanotechnology and biomedicine.  The presentation will conclude by addressing current challenges and future prospects in CNT research, emphasizing the ongoing need for collaborative efforts across multiple disciplines to fully harness their potential.
