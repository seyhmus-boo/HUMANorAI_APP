The fidelity of DNA replication, crucial for genetic stability, relies on the precise pairing of nucleotides.  This pairing is influenced by subtle quantum phenomena.  Firstly, the interaction between base pairs is governed by weak forces like van der Waals forces and hydrogen bonding, both having quantum mechanical origins.  Fluctuations in these forces, arising from zero-point energy and quantum tunneling, might influence base pair selection and contribute to occasional errors.
