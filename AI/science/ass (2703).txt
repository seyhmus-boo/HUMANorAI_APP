Carbon nanotubes (CNTs), cylindrical allotropes of carbon, exhibit exceptional mechanical, electrical, and thermal properties, making them a subject of intense interdisciplinary research.  This presentation will explore the key properties of CNTs, highlighting their remarkable strength and stiffness stemming from their spÂ² hybridized carbon structure, leading to applications in advanced composites and nano-electromechanical systems (NEMS).  We will discuss their unique electronic behavior, ranging from metallic to semiconducting depending on chirality, crucial for applications in electronics and sensors.  Furthermore, the presentation will address their high thermal conductivity, enabling advancements in thermal management and energy storage technologies.

The interdisciplinary nature of CNT research will be emphasized, showcasing connections between materials science, chemistry, physics, and engineering.  We will explore the challenges in large-scale production and functionalization of CNTs, including purification methods and surface modification techniques to tailor their properties for specific applications.  Finally, we will briefly outline current and future research directions, particularly focusing on the integration of CNTs into biomedical devices, flexible electronics, and sustainable energy solutions, highlighting the potential for transformative advancements across diverse fields.
