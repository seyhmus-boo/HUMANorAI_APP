Cloud computing, initially conceived as a utility computing model, has undergone a significant evolution since its inception.  Early iterations focused primarily on Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS), offering virtualized compute, storage, and networking resources.  This foundational layer paved the way for Platform as a Service (PaaS), providing development and deployment environments, and subsequently Software as a Service (SaaS), delivering ready-to-use applications directly to end-users.  This layered architecture has fostered innovation across diverse sectors.

Practical applications are ubiquitous.  In healthcare, cloud-based platforms facilitate secure data storage and remote patient monitoring.  Financial institutions leverage cloud infrastructure for high-performance transaction processing and fraud detection.  Furthermore,  the scalability and elasticity of cloud resources are crucial for e-commerce platforms handling fluctuating demand, and for scientific research demanding substantial computational power.
