5G networks represent a significant advancement in mobile communication technology, building upon the legacy of 1G's analog voice, 2G's digital voice and SMS, 3G's mobile data, and 4G's increased speed and LTE.  The key innovation of 5G lies in its utilization of higher frequency bands (millimeter waves) and advanced antenna technologies, such as massive MIMO, enabling drastically increased data rates, lower latency, and greater network capacity. This has profound implications across various technological sectors.

The enhanced speed and low latency facilitate the proliferation of Internet of Things (IoT) devices, enabling real-time data transmission crucial for applications like autonomous vehicles and smart city infrastructure.
