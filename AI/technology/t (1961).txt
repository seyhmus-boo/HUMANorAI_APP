Artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) are increasingly intertwined fields driving technological advancement. AI, broadly defined, encompasses the development of computer systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, reasoning, and problem-solving.  Machine learning, a subset of AI, focuses on enabling systems to learn from data without explicit programming. This learning occurs through algorithms that identify patterns, make predictions, and improve their performance over time based on the data they process.  The relationship is synergistic; AI provides the overarching goal of intelligent systems, while ML offers a powerful set of techniques to achieve that goal.  Practical applications are pervasive.  In healthcare, ML algorithms diagnose diseases from medical images with increasing accuracy, while in finance, they detect fraudulent transactions and predict market trends.  Autonomous vehicles rely heavily on ML for object recognition and navigation.
