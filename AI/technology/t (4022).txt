The rise of autonomous vehicles (AVs) represents a culmination of advancements across multiple technological domains.  Early conceptualizations emerged in the mid-20th century, with General Motors showcasing experimental automated systems as early as the 1950s. However, significant progress was hampered by limitations in computing power and sensor technology.  The late 20th and early 21st centuries witnessed crucial breakthroughs in areas such as artificial intelligence, computer vision, and GPS technology, providing the necessary building blocks for sophisticated AV systems.

The confluence of these advancements, coupled with increasing demand for improved road safety and traffic efficiency, has accelerated AV development.  Machine learning algorithms, capable of processing vast datasets to recognize patterns and predict behaviour, are central to AV navigation and decision-making.
