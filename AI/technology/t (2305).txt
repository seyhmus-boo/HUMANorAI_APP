Cloud computing has rapidly evolved from a niche technology to a ubiquitous paradigm shift in information technology, profoundly impacting various sectors. This paper examines the historical trajectory of cloud computing, tracing its evolution from early distributed computing concepts to its current multifaceted form.  We analyze key technological advancements that fueled this transformation, focusing on the pivotal roles played by virtualization, software-defined networking, and containerization.  Furthermore, the study explores the shifting service models, from Infrastructure-as-a-Service (IaaS) to Platform-as-a-Service (PaaS) and Software-as-a-Service (SaaS), and their respective impacts on application development, deployment, and management.  The paper also investigates the influence of emerging technologies such as serverless computing, edge computing, and artificial intelligence on the future trajectory of cloud computing.
