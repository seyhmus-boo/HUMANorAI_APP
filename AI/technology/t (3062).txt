Blockchain technology, initially conceived in 1991 by Stuart Haber and W. Scott Stornetta for timestamping documents and further developed in 2008 with the advent of Bitcoin by Satoshi Nakamoto, has evolved beyond its cryptographic currency origins to encompass a broad spectrum of applications.  Its core functionality lies in the distributed ledger architecture, enabling secure and transparent recording of transactions across a decentralized network.  This eliminates the need for a central authority, enhancing data integrity and resilience against single points of failure.  Applications currently span diverse sectors, including supply chain management, where blockchain enhances traceability and authenticity; healthcare, facilitating secure and interoperable patient data management; and finance, offering improvements in cross-border payments and asset tokenization.  Furthermore, its potential in digital identity verification, voting systems, and intellectual property rights management is being actively explored.
