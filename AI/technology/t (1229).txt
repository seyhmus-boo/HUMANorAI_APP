Artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML), while often used interchangeably, represent distinct yet interconnected fields.  AI, encompassing the broader goal of creating intelligent agents, utilizes ML as a crucial subset. ML, focusing on algorithms that enable systems to learn from data without explicit programming, underpins numerous practical applications.  This review highlights the transformative impact of these technologies across diverse sectors.

In healthcare, ML algorithms power diagnostic tools, predicting disease progression and personalizing treatment plans.  Finance leverages AI for fraud detection, algorithmic trading, and risk assessment.  The manufacturing industry benefits from AI-driven predictive maintenance, optimizing production efficiency and minimizing downtime.  Furthermore, self-driving vehicles rely heavily on ML for object recognition and navigational decision-making.

Despite their potential, challenges remain.  Concerns regarding data bias, algorithmic transparency, and ethical implications necessitate careful consideration.  Further research is needed to address these issues and unlock the full potential of AI and ML, ensuring responsible development and deployment of these powerful technologies.  Overall, the rapid advancements in AI and ML are revolutionizing numerous industries, promising significant advancements across various sectors.
