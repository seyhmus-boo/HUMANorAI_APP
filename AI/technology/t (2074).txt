This presentation provides a concise overview of recent key developments in Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML).  We will explore advancements beyond traditional supervised and unsupervised learning, focusing on the burgeoning fields of reinforcement learning and deep learning.  Recent breakthroughs in reinforcement learning, particularly in areas like robotics and game playing, will be discussed, highlighting the efficacy of novel reward function designs and improved algorithmic efficiency.  Furthermore, the presentation will examine advancements in deep learning architectures, such as transformers and graph neural networks, and their applications in natural language processing (NLP) and complex data analysis.  We will address the increasing prevalence of large language models (LLMs) and their impact on various domains, including healthcare, finance, and scientific research.  Finally, we will briefly discuss the ethical considerations surrounding the rapid advancement of AI/ML, including issues of bias, transparency, and accountability, emphasizing the need for responsible development and deployment.  The presentation aims to offer a balanced perspective on the exciting potential and inherent challenges of these rapidly evolving technologies.
