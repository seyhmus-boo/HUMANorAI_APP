The proliferation of 5G networks represents a significant technological leap, promising unprecedented speeds and connectivity.  While the impact of this advancement is typically analyzed through classical information theory and engineering perspectives, this paper explores the nascent intersection of 5G technology and quantum mechanics.  The dramatically increased data rates afforded by 5G necessitate a reevaluation of traditional communication models, potentially opening avenues for quantum-enhanced technologies.  This investigation focuses on how the high bandwidth and low latency characteristics of 5G networks could facilitate the development and deployment of quantum communication protocols, including quantum key distribution (QKD) and quantum teleportation.  We will examine the challenges posed by the current 5G infrastructure in supporting quantum information transmission, such as noise and decoherence, and analyze potential solutions.  Furthermore, we will discuss the potential role of 5G in advancing quantum computing by enabling the efficient exchange of quantum information between geographically dispersed quantum processors.
