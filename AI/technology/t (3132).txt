Artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) have rapidly transitioned from theoretical concepts to transformative technologies impacting numerous facets of modern life.  This paper explores the intertwined evolution and current applications of AI and ML, emphasizing their synergistic relationship.  The conceptual foundations of AI can be traced back to the Dartmouth Workshop of 1956, marking a pivotal moment where the term was coined and early research programs were initiated.  Initial efforts focused on symbolic reasoning and rule-based systems, aiming to replicate human cognitive processes through explicitly programmed logic. However, limitations in handling complex, real-world data became apparent.  The subsequent rise of ML, particularly with the advent of readily available computational power and large datasets, offered a powerful alternative. ML algorithms, focusing on statistical learning from data rather than explicit programming, enabled the development of systems capable of pattern recognition, prediction, and decision-making with increasing sophistication.
