Cloud computing, a paradigm shift in information technology, has rapidly evolved from its nascent stages to become a ubiquitous force shaping modern digital landscapes.  This paper examines the historical trajectory of cloud computing, tracing its development from early distributed computing models to the sophisticated, service-oriented architectures prevalent today.  We analyze key technological advancements, including virtualization, containerization, and serverless computing, that have fueled this evolution and enabled the proliferation of diverse cloud service models, such as Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS), Platform as a Service (PaaS), and Software as a Service (SaaS).  The impact of this evolution is profound, manifested in the widespread adoption of cloud-based solutions across numerous sectors.  Practical applications range from facilitating scalable e-commerce platforms and powering sophisticated artificial intelligence algorithms to enabling remote work and enhancing healthcare delivery through electronic health records management.
