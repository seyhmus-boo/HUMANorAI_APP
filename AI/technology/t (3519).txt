Fifth-generation (5G) networks represent a significant advancement in wireless communication technology, characterized by significantly increased bandwidth, reduced latency, and enhanced network capacity compared to their predecessors. This enhanced performance profoundly impacts numerous technological sectors.  The ultra-low latency of 5G facilitates the development of real-time applications such as autonomous vehicles, remote surgery, and industrial automation, enabling precise control and immediate feedback loops crucial for these systems.  The high bandwidth empowers the proliferation of data-intensive applications like high-definition video streaming, augmented and virtual reality experiences, and the Internet of Things (IoT), supporting a massive interconnected network of devices.
