Antibiotic resistance (AR), the ability of bacteria to withstand antimicrobial treatment, poses a significant threat to global health, akin to an escalating arms race between humans and microbial adversaries.  Like a fortress continuously upgrading its defenses, bacteria develop resistance mechanisms through mutations and horizontal gene transfer, rendering previously effective antibiotics obsolete. This process is accelerated by the overuse and misuse of antibiotics in human and veterinary medicine, analogous to providing the enemy with blueprints for stronger fortifications.  The consequences are far-reaching, resembling a domino effect: increased morbidity and mortality from infections, prolonged hospital stays, and escalating healthcare costs.  Furthermore, the impact extends beyond direct patient care, threatening food security through compromised livestock production and impacting global economies.  Addressing AR necessitates a multi-pronged approach analogous to a coordinated military campaign: optimizing antibiotic stewardship, developing novel antimicrobial therapies, and investing in advanced diagnostics.
