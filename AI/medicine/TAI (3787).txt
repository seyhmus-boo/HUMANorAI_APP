Historically, adolescent mental health was largely overlooked, with societal stigma and a lack of specialized services hindering early identification and intervention.  However, recent decades have witnessed a significant increase in research and awareness surrounding mental health disorders in this population.  Trends reveal rising rates of anxiety, depression, and self-harm, particularly among girls, often linked to societal pressures, academic stress, and increased social media usage.  Eating disorders and substance abuse also remain prevalent.

Treatment approaches have evolved alongside this growing understanding. Furthermore, a move toward holistic approaches, incorporating family therapy, peer support groups, and mindfulness techniques, recognizes the interconnectedness of biological, psychological, and social factors influencing adolescent mental well-being.  Early intervention programs and school-based mental health services are gaining traction, aiming for preventative care and reducing the long-term impact of untreated disorders.  Despite progress, significant challenges remain, including access to affordable and quality care, particularly for underserved populations, and the need for further research on the long-term efficacy of various treatments.
