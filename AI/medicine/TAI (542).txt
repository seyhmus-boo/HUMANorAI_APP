Telemedicine, the remote delivery of healthcare services using information and communication technologies, has undergone a dramatic evolution, significantly reshaping modern healthcare delivery.  Recent developments underscore its increasing importance, driven by technological advancements and evolving healthcare needs.  High-speed internet access and the proliferation of smartphones have facilitated wider adoption of remote patient monitoring (RPM) technologies, enabling continuous data collection on vital signs and other health metrics.  This allows for proactive intervention and improved disease management, particularly for chronic conditions like diabetes and heart failure.  Furthermore, advancements in video conferencing and telehealth platforms have enhanced the accessibility of specialist consultations, bridging geographical barriers and improving access to care in underserved areas.  Artificial intelligence (AI) is also playing a growing role, powering diagnostic tools and facilitating automated analysis of medical images, enhancing diagnostic accuracy and efficiency.  However, challenges remain, including ensuring data security and patient privacy, addressing issues of digital literacy and equity of access, and establishing clear regulatory frameworks to govern the burgeoning field.
