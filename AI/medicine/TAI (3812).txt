Telemedicine, the remote delivery of healthcare services using information and communication technologies, has emerged as a transformative force in modern healthcare delivery.  Its impact stems from its ability to overcome geographical barriers, enhance accessibility, and improve efficiency within existing healthcare systems.  Practical applications are diverse and expanding rapidly.  For instance, asynchronous telemedicine utilizes store-and-forward technology for the transmission of medical images and patient records to specialists, facilitating timely diagnoses in underserved areas lacking onsite expertise.  Synchronous applications, such as real-time video consultations, enable remote monitoring of chronic conditions like diabetes and hypertension, providing patients with regular check-ups and personalized guidance.  Furthermore, telepsychiatry offers vital mental health support to individuals in isolated communities or those with mobility challenges.  The integration of telehealth with wearable sensors enables continuous data collection, allowing for proactive intervention and preventative care.
