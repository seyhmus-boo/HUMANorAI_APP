Furthermore, the process raises concerns about autonomy and informed consent.  The inherent vulnerability of potential donors, particularly those in critical condition or deceased, demands rigorous scrutiny.  Obtaining consent for organ donation resembles obtaining informed consent for a risky medical procedure; the potential benefits must outweigh the risks, and the decision must be freely made without coercion, mirroring the ethical framework surrounding clinical trials.

Finally, the potential for exploitation and inequality necessitates careful consideration.  Financial incentives for organ donation, analogous to a black market for blood, risk exacerbating existing socio-economic disparities and creating a system where the wealthy secure organs while the poor are exploited.  Establishing equitable and transparent organ allocation systems is crucial to navigate these treacherous ethical shoals.
